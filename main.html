Santorini, Greece


Santorini is one of the most famous Cyclades islands in the Aegean Sea. It was devastated by a volcanic eruption in the 16th century BC, forever shaping its rugged landscape. The island is world-renowned for its whitewashed houses carved into the cliffs, its breathtaking sunsets, and the deep blue waters of its giant submerged caldera.

Top 5 Attractions

  1. The Oia Sunset: Watching the sun dip below the horizon from the northern tip of the island is an unforgettable experience.
  2. The Red Beach: Famous for its unique volcanic sand and soaring red lava cliffs.
  3. Ancient Akrotiri: A prehistoric city preserved in volcanic ash, often referred to as the "Minoan Pompeii."
  4. Fira to Oia Hike: A scenic cliffside trail that offers the most stunning views of the caldera.
  5. Amoudi Bay: A charming port at the base of Oia, perfect for cliff jumping and fresh seafood.

Must-Try Foods

  • Tomatokeftedes: These are delicious tomato fritters made with local cherry tomatoes and herbs.
  • Fava Me Koukia: A creamy yellow split pea puree that is a staple of Santorini cuisine.
  • Chlorotyri: A local sour goat cheese that is incredibly rare and creamy.
  • Saganaki: Mouth-watering fried cheese wrapped in filo pastry and drizzled with honey.
